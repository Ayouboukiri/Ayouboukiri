What is Chess

Chess is a two-player strategy board game that is played on an 8x8 grid called a chessboard. It is one of the most popular and enduring games in the world, known for its deep complexity and strategic depth. The game is played between two opponents, often referred to as "white" and "black," who take turns moving their pieces with the goal of capturing the opponent's king. The player who puts the opponent's king in a position from which it cannot escape is said to have achieved checkmate, and they win the game.

Objective

The primary objective of chess is to checkmate your opponent's king . This means putting the king in a position where it is under attack and cannot escape capture.
